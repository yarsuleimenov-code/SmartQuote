Lifecycle Risks

Architecture risks that the lifecycle separation is designed to prevent.

Critical controls

Direct Estimate Editing

Severity: High

Editing a sent estimate makes historical pricing unreliable.

Mitigation: any change creates a new draft and a new estimate version.

Order Changes Pricing

Severity: High

Operational execution must not recalculate approved commercial pricing.

Mitigation: order uses the frozen estimate snapshot.

eBOL Without Snapshot

Severity: Medium

Field verification can lose connection to the original item list.

Mitigation: eBOL is created from order and frozen item data.

Lifecycle Overload

Severity: Medium

Putting dispatch and eBOL logic into quote creation slows broker work.

Mitigation: calculator creates quotes; operational screens execute delivery.

BA Notes

Short architecture notes for product and business review.

Snapshot Integrity

Historical estimates must not recalculate after changes to fuel variables, formulas, pricing logic, or route rules.

Commercial vs Operational Separation

Calculator owns quote and estimate lifecycle. Orders and eBOL own dispatch, pickup, delivery, POD, and exceptions.

Long-term Architecture Goal

Pricing should move to a governed backend engine, while every lifecycle transition remains auditable and reproducible.
